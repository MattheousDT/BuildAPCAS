What you will need

  • A Motherboard
  • A Processor
  • CPU Heatsink Fan (You can use the one with your processor if you want)
  • RAM sticks
  • A Hard Drive
  • An SSD (Optional)
  • A Graphics Card
  • A Wireless Card (Optional)
  • Power Supply
  • A Phillips Head Screwdriver
  • Something to hold you screws in (We recommend an ice cube tray as you can seperate the different screws)
 

And around an hour to 2 hours of your time

Step 1 - Installing the CPU

 

Lift the socket cover using the retention arm on the right side of the socket.

Insert the CPU matching the triangle on the CPU to the Triangle on the motherboard/socket

Step 2 - Installing the RAM

 

Pull the tabs on the end of the slots outwards

Then insert the sticks by matching the pushing downwards into the slot

The tabs should snap back, if they do not, you can push them back and the ram should click in place.

Step 3 - Installing the CPU Heatsink

 

Consult the manual on the heatsink as all are different.

Generally speaking. Install the backplate on the back of the motherboard and install screws through the holes on the motherboard.

Apply thermal paste onto the CPU. The recommended amount is about the size of a pea. This is due to it being spread when you apply the weight of the heatsink onto the processor.

Then attach the fan to the heatsink and plug it into the CPU fan header on the motherboard.
